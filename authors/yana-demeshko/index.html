Nuclear Arms Control during the Cold War

The history of strategic nuclear arms control agreements between the U.S. and Russia, who today own approximately 91% of all nuclear warheads in the world, began on May 26, 1972 with the sighining of SALT I (Strategic Arms Limitation Talks) in Moscow by then President Richard Nixon and General Secretary Leonid Brezhnev.  

Now for over half a century, the two countries have engaged in numerous dialogues to faciliate nuclear arms reductions, making the issue of arms control an area for potential successful bilateral cooperation even though today the U.S. and Russia are experiencing arguably the worst relations since the end of the Cold War.
